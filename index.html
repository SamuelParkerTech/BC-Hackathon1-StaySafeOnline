Are you Safe Online?

A guide to staying safe online, best practices and further resources to keep you protected.

What are the dangers we face online?

Browsing online and the internet is fantastic but at the same time there are many dangers and potential risks from being online. These can range from scams to privacy worries to bullying. Inappropriate content and illegal activities.


Unsafe/Fake Websites

Fake websites can pose as real ones to steal your personal data and or credit cards. These often take the form of fake shops or ticket websites. Check for a HTTPS in the website url.

Phishing Emails

Emails purporting to be from a reliable source but often spoof the address. These can be attempts to gain your passwords, credit details or if some cases make false bank payments. Always check the sender address.

Whatsapp Scams

These are more recent and modern scams, usually someone pretending to be someone you know especially a loved one. They pretend to have lost a phone number and this is their 'new number', they then ask you to send them money to an account.

Virus and Malware

Another way you are at risk online is via virus and malware. These are often in suspicious emails with dodgy attachments, or on fake websites with malicious downloads. These can be rather difficult to remove and vigilance is the best defence as well as anti-virus software such as Windows Defender or Malware Bytes.

Take a look our Best Practices page for tips on how to stay safe online. We also have a list of other websites and helpful organisations on our resources page.
