Ways To Stay Safe

Some best practices to keep you protected.

Best Practice Online


Practicing online safety is essential to protect your personal information and digital identity. Always use strong, unique passwords for each of your accounts and enable two-factor authentication whenever possible. Be cautious when sharing personal details on social media and avoid clicking on suspicious links or downloading attachments from unknown sources. Regularly update your software and devices to protect against the latest security threats. Additionally, using a reputable antivirus program and a secure, encrypted connection (VPN) can further enhance your online security. Stay informed about potential scams and phishing attempts, and always verify the legitimacy of websites before entering sensitive information.

Tips For Online Safety

Suggested by The National Cyber Security Centre


  • Protect your email by using a strong and separate password
  • Cyber criminals can use your email to access many of your personal accounts, leaving you vulnerable to identity theft.
  • Install the latest software and app updates
  • Software and app updates contain vital security updates to help protect your devices from cyber criminals.
  • Turn on 2-Factor Authentication (2FA)
  • 2-Factor Authentication is recommended to help protect your online accounts.
  • Password managers: how they help you secure passwords
  • Using a password manager can help you create and remember passwords.
  • Backing up your data
  • Safeguard your most important data, such as your photos and key documents, by backing them up to an external hard drive or a cloud-based storage system.
  • Three random words
  • Use three random words to create a password that's difficult to crack.

Other Safety Tips


Ensure your internet connection is secure, especially when using public Wi-Fi. Be cautious about what you download, and choose strong, unique passwords for your accounts. When making online purchases, only use secure, reputable sites. Additionally, be mindful of what you post online, as it can be difficult to remove once shared.

Be aware of online scams targeting platforms like WhatsApp. Scammers use phishing tactics, sending deceptive messages that appear legitimate to steal personal information. Romance scams are also prevalent; fraudsters create fake profiles to form emotional bonds and then solicit money with fabricated stories. Users should stay vigilant, verify contacts, and avoid sharing sensitive information with strangers online.
